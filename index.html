Paw Me

a wide variety of famous cat breeds

Persia

Persian cats are a long-haired domestic cat breed characterized by a round face and short muzzle.

Anggora

The Turkish Anggora is one of the oldest natural domestic cat breeds. The breed originated in Ankara, Turkey.

Siberia

Siberian cats are one of the natural domestic cat breeds native to Siberia, Russia.

Maine Coon

Maine coons are one of the oldest and most natural cat breeds that originated in Maine, United States.

Siam

Siamese cats were one of the first cat breeds to be clearly recognized as oriental.

Munchkin

Munchkin cats are one of the short-legged cat breeds formed due to natural genetic mutations.

Himalaya

Himalayan cats are one of the domestic cat breeds resulting from a cross between a Persian cat and a Siamese cat.

Ragdoll

Ragdolls are one of the largest cat breeds in the world and have half-length hair with blue eyes.

Take care of your lovely cat

Make them feel loved and at home

Foods

Provide a nutritious and balanced diet according to your cat's needs on a regular basis, such as foods that contain taurine and essential amino acids for eye and heart health. Then avoid foods that can interfere with your cat's health.

Grooming

Comb your cat's coat daily to prevent tangles or dreadlocks, especially in long-haired cats. Take your cat to pet care for professional grooming once a month.

Dental Care

Regularly check your cat's gums and teeth for dental problems. Take your cat to the vet to check your cat's dental health if you have any doubts.

Parasite Control

Give your cat deworming medication every 3 months to prevent worms from developing and flea drops to your cat's entire body once a month to prevent flea infestation.

Vaccination

Vaccinate your cat once a year with rabies, FVRCP and FeLV vaccines if your cat is under 1 year old and then once a year thereafter.

Sterilization

Sterilization is not required, but if you want your cat to avoid wandering far away where they may contract diseases, and want your cat to be calm and your home to be free of territory markings, neutering may be recommended.
